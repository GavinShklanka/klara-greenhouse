The Problem
The ecosystem exists.
The routing layer is missing.

Nova Scotia already has growers, markets, advisers, food hubs, buy-local incentives, applied research capacity, and commercialization programs. What is missing is the routing layer that helps people act inside that ecosystem.

  • Growers produce without visibility into where demand actually is — excess spoils or sells below value.
  • Buyers know what they need but cannot easily find local supply at the right time, volume, and compliance level.
  • Households and new growers face a fragmented decision path from "I want to grow" to "I know what to build, buy, and plant."

The problem is not lack of capacity. It is lack of structured, locally-grounded decision support.
