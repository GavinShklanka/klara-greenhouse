# Who Benefits

## Klara creates value for every participant in the ecosystem

### Growers

Small commercial operators get clearer visibility into demand, viable
channels, and next actions based on their actual constraints. Transition
growers get a structured path from intention to viable operation.
Excess becomes revenue, not waste.

### Households and Prosumers

A trusted entry point into a confusing category. Viable structure,
realistic budget, seasonal plan, and vetted supplier path — matched
to your site, not generic national defaults.

### Restaurants and Food Service

Forward-demand declaration translated into local grower-actionable
signals. Your procurement gets more predictable local supply. Growers
get forward visibility. Both sides plan better.

### Institutions and Retailers

Compliance-aware matching for institutional food-service contracts
and retail supply. Klara surfaces documentation requirements and
routes qualified growers to qualified buyers.

### Market Networks and Food Hubs

Inventory-gap filling and overflow routing. When growers have excess,
Klara identifies markets that need it. The network gets stronger
because supply rebalancing is structured, not ad hoc.

### Advisory and Extension Bodies

Klara routes users to extension at the right moment — not too early,
not too late. Better-prepared users. More productive interactions.
Coordination, not replacement.

### Community-Food Partners

A stronger market-channel network benefits community-food programs
downstream. Klara keeps small commercials financially healthy,
routes surplus into viable channels, and prevents produce
death-in-field. The conditions for community-food partnerships
improve because the ecosystem is healthier.
