# Klara Greenhouse — Founder Brief

## What it is

A decision-support routing layer for protected growing and local food
commercialization in Nova Scotia. One core expressed five ways:
small-commercial improvement, prosumer enablement, forward-demand
declaration, partner-channeled routing, and excess-supply rebalancing.

## What it is not

Not a listing site. Not a marketplace. Not a replacement for Perennia,
NSCC, or extension services. Not an automated recommendation engine.
Klara suggests viable paths with tradeoffs. Humans decide.

## Why Nova Scotia, why now

428,000+ dwellings. 2,741 farms. 30.3% direct sales. 100+ greenhouse
growers. 40+ FMNS markets. Active buy-local, food-safety, and
commercialization programs. The ecosystem exists. The routing layer
does not.

## Current stage

Validation-stage prototype. Working structured intake, archetype
matching, and proposal generation. Seeking PIVP Tier 1 support through
NSCC for applied validation with real users.

## Key relationships

Aligned with NSCC applied-research pathways, designed to route users
toward Perennia-style advisory escalation, and compatible with Nova
Scotia Loyal and FMNS market-access goals.

## Commercialization sequence

Local excess routing → recurring direct channels → institutional and
food-service readiness → retail readiness → export readiness.
Each stage builds the trust and compliance proof the next requires.

## The ask

Fit check. Role clarity. Support structure. Formal backing pathway.
All concrete. No vague requests.

## Territory acknowledgment

Operates on unceded Mi'kmaw territory (Mi'kma'ki). Peace and Friendship
Treaties of 1725–1779. No Mi'kmaw partnership currently exists;
engagement held as prospective, awaiting institutional introduction.
