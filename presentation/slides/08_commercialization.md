# Commercialization & Excess Supply

## Turning overflow into revenue, systematically

Klara helps identify market gaps, nearby buyer channels, food-hub pathways, CSA/direct routes, institutional readiness, and eventual export-readiness progression.

---

<div class="section-divider"><span>The Five-Stage Sequence</span></div>

### 1. Local Excess Routing (Now)
Identify FMNS member markets, online ordering hubs, and food-hub partners with current inventory gaps in the grower's produce category. Same-week market rebalancing.

### 2. Recurring Direct Channels (Near-term)
Match household and small-buyer demand with grower capacity for CSA and farm-share models that create predictable shoulder-season cash flow.

### 3. Institutional Readiness (Medium-term)
Restaurants, hospitals, and food-service distributors with forward-committed demand. Klara routes growers who meet documentation and compliance requirements to these buyers.

### 4. Retail Readiness (Medium-to-later-term)
Nova Scotia Loyal and major retailers (Sobeys, Loblaw, Walmart). Klara surfaces retail compliance requirements and routes qualified growers to qualified shelf programmes.

### 5. Export Readiness (Later-stage)
Help growers aggregate volume, prove reliability, and graduate into formal export channels.

---

> *Each stage builds the trust, compliance proof, and operational reliability that the next stage requires. Klara's value compounds across the sequence.*

## Speaker Notes

- "Export Readiness" is the long-term vision, but it's gated by compliance and scale. Klara guides growers through the steps to get there.
- Focus heavily on Stage 1 (Now): we have the infrastructure (FMNS, Cape Breton Food Hub, WFM2Go). We just need the routing intelligence.
- Emphasize that Klara doesn't manage deliveries; it routes the connection.
