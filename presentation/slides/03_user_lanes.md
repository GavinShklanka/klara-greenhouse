# What Klara Does

## Five distinct functions. One decision-support core.

Klara is not a listing site. It suggests feasible paths through five functions: grower improvement, prosumer setup, forward-demand declaration, supplier/advisory routing, and excess-supply commercialization.

---

### 1 · Grower Improvement
**Small commercial operators.** Given current scale, inventory, and season, Klara suggests specific next actions grounded in evidence (e.g., sell excess to a specific market, upgrade structure, expand channel).

### 2 · Prosumer Setup
**Households and new growers.** A structured path from intention to confident decision: viable structure, realistic budget, seasonal plan, and vetted supplier path matched to their site.

### 3 · Forward-Demand Declaration
**Restaurants, institutions, retailers.** Buyers declare what they need. Klara translates demand into grower-actionable signals. This is the inverse of a marketplace — demand routed to supply.

### 4 · Supplier & Advisory Routing
**Ecosystem partners.** Klara routes users to vetted partners at the right moment: Perennia for agronomic advice, NSCC for applied research, Halifax Seed for inputs, Greenhouse NS for structures.

### 5 · Excess-Supply Commercialization
**Market networks & food hubs.** When a grower has overflow, Klara routes it into channels with inventory gaps (FMNS member markets, WFM2Go, Cape Breton Food Hub).

---

> *Klara is not a marketplace. No transactions clear through the platform. Klara suggests; humans decide.*
