# The Problem

## Nova Scotia has the ecosystem. It is missing the routing layer.

People who want to grow under protection in Nova Scotia face one specific problem: **decision friction across a fragmented environment.**

---

<div class="section-divider"><span>What the friction looks like</span></div>

- Generic online advice that does not account for Nova Scotia climate, costs, or suppliers
- Households and operators who do not know which structure, scale, or partner fits their site, budget, and intent
- Conversations with local suppliers that start too late — after confusion is already baked in
- A widening gap between *"I want to grow more"* and *"I know what to buy and from whom"*

---

<div class="section-divider"><span>Why this matters</span></div>

Most people facing this gap end up in one of three places:

1. **Over-buy** — purchase infrastructure that does not match readiness or use case
2. **Under-buy** — start too small and either abandon or face expensive mid-season changes
3. **Don't start** — because the path from intention to confident decision feels unclear

---

<div class="section-divider"><span>What the market currently offers</span></div>

| Source | What it provides | What it misses |
|---|---|---|
| Manufacturer websites | Specs and sizes | Local fit, climate, readiness <span class="ev-badge ev-A">A</span> |
| Garden centres | General advice | Structured decision support <span class="ev-badge ev-A">A</span> |
| Online forums | Anecdotes | Consistency, local grounding <span class="ev-badge ev-B">B</span> |
| Consultants | Expertise | Accessibility, cost <span class="ev-badge ev-A">A</span> |

---

> *The problem is not lack of information. The problem is the absence of a routing layer that connects intent to a viable next action through the network Nova Scotia already has.*

## Speaker Notes

- "Decision friction across a fragmented environment" is the v1.0.4-candidate problem framing. Use it.
- The three outcome buckets (over-buy / under-buy / don't start) are what Klara routing interrupts.
- If they push on "why not just talk to a supplier": that's part of what Klara routes toward — but supplier conversations work best after a confident frame is built. Klara builds the frame.
- The table is for the audience to scan, not to be read aloud. Move past it once they have it.

## Do Not Say

- Do not say the market is "broken" — it's underserved, which is different.
- Do not claim no tools exist. Say they don't serve this specific need.
- Do not use "pain points" — overused, weakens the observation.
