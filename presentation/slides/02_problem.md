# The Problem

## Nova Scotia has the ecosystem. It is missing the routing layer.

Nova Scotia has growers, suppliers, markets, training, advisory bodies, and buy-local programs, but the ecosystem is fragmented. Klara helps users move from intent to viable next step.

---

<div class="section-divider"><span>What the friction looks like</span></div>

- **Growers** produce without visibility into where demand actually is — excess spoils or sells below value.
- **Buyers** (restaurants, institutions, retailers) know what they need but cannot easily find local supply at the right time, volume, and compliance level.
- **Households and new growers** face a fragmented decision path from "I want to grow" to "I know what to build, buy, and plant".

---

<div class="section-divider"><span>Why this matters</span></div>

Because there is no structured decision support, the friction leads to:

1. **Wasted commercial capacity** — surplus food doesn't reach the markets that need it
2. **Disconnected supply chains** — institutional demand cannot easily signal local producers
3. **Stalled household ambition** — prosumers over-buy, under-buy, or abandon intent

---

> *The problem is not lack of capacity. It is lack of structured, locally-grounded decision support.*
