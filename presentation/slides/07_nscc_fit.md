# Why NSCC

## A partnership-channeled validation route — not a co-founder

NSCC's fit here is specific, valuable, and bounded. In v1.0.4-candidate framing, NSCC is one of the partnership channels Function 4 routes through.

---

<div class="section-divider"><span>What NSCC brings to the routing</span></div>

**User-testing access** — NSCC programs in agriculture, horticulture, and related disciplines represent Function 1 (small commercial) and Function 2 (prosumer) users in structured environments. People who are already thinking about growing at scale.

**Intake pressure-testing** — NSCC can support structured walkthroughs of the intake flow with students, faculty, and program participants who have real domain knowledge.

**Prototype-to-pilot routing** — Institutional engagement turns the routing prototype into a credibly tested tool. That's what PIVP validation requires.

**Service-provider credibility** — NSCC affiliation signals to suppliers, growers, and future users that the routing has been put through real scrutiny, not just founder testing.

---

<div class="section-divider"><span>What this partnership is NOT</span></div>

| Not this | Why it matters to say it |
|---|---|
| Co-founder or co-owner role | NSCC is a validation channel partner, not an equity holder |
| Product development lead | Product direction stays with the founder |
| Ongoing operational dependency | The routing is designed to be independently operated |
| Academic research project | Applied validation for a live product |

---

<div class="section-divider"><span>What a successful NSCC engagement looks like</span></div>

1. Structured intake walkthroughs with program participants (Function 1 + Function 2)
2. Debrief sessions to surface vocabulary and routing-logic gaps
3. Formal letter of support / institutional sign-off for PIVP application
4. Optional: continued engagement as a stakeholder as the routing matures
