# Why NSCC

## An applied validation partner — not a co-founder

NSCC's fit here is specific, valuable, and bounded.

---

### What NSCC brings to this project

**User-testing access**
NSCC programs in agriculture, horticulture, and related disciplines represent Lane 1 and Lane 2 users in structured environments — people who are already thinking about growing at scale.

**Usability and intake pressure-testing**
NSCC can support structured walkthroughs of the intake flow with students, faculty, and program participants who have real domain knowledge.

**Prototype-to-pilot support**
Institutional engagement turns a prototype into a credibly tested tool — which is what PIVP validation requires.

**Service-provider credibility**
An NSCC affiliation signals to suppliers, growers, and future users that this tool has been put through real scrutiny, not just founder testing.

---

### What this partnership is NOT

| Not this | Why it matters to say it |
|----------|--------------------------|
| Co-founder or co-owner role | NSCC is a validation partner, not an equity holder |
| Product development lead | The product direction stays with the founder |
| Ongoing operational dependency | The tool is designed to be independently operated |
| Academic research project | This is applied validation for a live product |

---

### What a successful NSCC engagement looks like

1. Structured intake walkthroughs with program participants
2. Debrief sessions to surface vocabulary and recommendation gaps
3. Formal letter of support or institutional sign-off for PIVP application
4. Optional: continued engagement as a stakeholder as the product matures

---

> *NSCC's role is to make the validation rigorous. The product belongs to the founder.*

## Speaker Notes

- Lead with what NSCC gets out of this: applied partnership, student engagement with a real tool, sector relevance.
- Be explicit about role boundaries early — it prevents scope creep in the conversation.
- If they ask about ongoing involvement: "We'd welcome that conversation after Layer 1 is closed — but we want to be clear that the current ask is for structured support during this validation phase."
- The letter of support is a concrete and low-burden ask. Surface it directly.

<!-- do_not_say -->
## Do Not Say

- Do not imply NSCC would have product or IP ownership.
- Do not promise ongoing academic collaboration unless you're prepared to deliver it.
- Do not overstate the depth of current relationship — if it's introductory, say so.
