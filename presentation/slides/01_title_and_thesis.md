# Klara Greenhouse

## A Nova Scotia decision-support engine for protected growing

---

**The problem in one sentence:**
Households and serious prosumers in Nova Scotia cannot confidently choose a protected-growing path because generic information doesn't account for their actual site, budget, crop intent, or readiness.

**The one-sentence test (Landscape v1.0.3):**
Klara does not list products to people. It listens to what a person or operator actually needs, and suggests the optimal path based on what's actually viable in Nova Scotia — using the existing food and agriculture network.

**The ten-word test:**
Suggests viable paths through Nova Scotia's protected-growing decision environment.

---

> *This is a validation-stage project. Everything here reflects what exists and what is being proven, not where we expect to land in three years. Reference: Klara Landscape v1.0.3 (2026-04-24).*

---

**Territory acknowledgment.** Klara Greenhouse operates on the ancestral and unceded Mi'kmaw territory of Mi'kma'ki, governed by the Peace and Friendship Treaties of 1725–1779, which did not cede land and remain in force. Klara does not claim to advance Mi'kmaw food sovereignty; engagement with Mi'kmaw-led initiatives is held as prospective, awaiting institutional introduction.

## Speaker Notes

- Anchor the room immediately: this is a Nova Scotia-specific tool, not a generic gardening app.
- "Decision-support engine" replaces "routing and proposal engine" per Landscape v1.0.3 — the language now emphasizes that Klara suggests viable paths rather than producing a single recommendation.
- The one-sentence and ten-word tests come verbatim from Landscape v1.0.3 § Plain-language tests; use them as your plain-English anchors.
- The territory acknowledgment is protocol-critical and should be read or referenced honestly. Do not skip it. Do not paraphrase the "unceded Mi'kmaw territory" or "Klara does not claim" phrasings — they are load-bearing.
- If asked about Mi'kmaw partnerships: refer to the v1.0.3 Mi'kma'ki section. Klara has no current Mi'kmaw partnership and will not cold-contact; any engagement requires institutional introduction (NSCC, Perennia, SMU, MITACS).
- The thesis is narrow on purpose. Narrow is credible at this stage.
- If they ask about national expansion: "That's not the conversation we're having today. This is about proving local fit first."

## Do Not Say

- Do not say "platform" in the opening, it signals scope creep before you've earned it.
- Do not open with a market size number. It's not where this conversation starts.
- Do not mention AI, machine learning, or automation in slide 1.
- Do not soften "unceded Mi'kmaw territory" to "traditional territory" — unceded is load-bearing under the Peace and Friendship Treaties.
- Do not claim Klara serves Mi'kmaw communities. Klara does not claim to advance Mi'kmaw food sovereignty.
