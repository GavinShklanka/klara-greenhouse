# Klara Greenhouse

## Routing infrastructure for Nova Scotia's protected-growing decision environment

---

**The thesis in one sentence (grandfather test):**

> Klara doesn't list products to people — it listens to what a person or operator actually needs, and routes them to the right next action through our existing Nova Scotia food and agriculture network.

**The thesis in ten words:**

> Routes intake through Nova Scotia's existing food and agriculture network.

## Speaker Notes

- **Anchor: routing infrastructure, not a listing site.** This is the load-bearing reframe in v1.0.4-candidate language. Pat will hear the difference if he saw the prior deck.
- The grandfather test is the plain-language anchor for any non-technical NS reader. Use it if Pat's body language reads "what does this actually do."
- Hybrid verb discipline: routes for infrastructure framing; suggests for the inner decision-support discipline. Both true, both load-bearing.
- Territory acknowledgment is protocol-critical and read literally. Do not paraphrase. Do not soften "unceded." Do not soften "Klara does not claim to advance."
- If asked about Mi'kmaw partnerships: Klara has none currently and will not cold-contact. Engagement requires institutional introduction (NSCC, Perennia, SMU, MITACS).
- If asked about national expansion: "That's not the conversation today. Local proof first."

<!-- do_not_say -->
## Do Not Say

- Do not say "platform" — scope-creep tell.
- Do not open with a market size number.
- Do not soften "unceded Mi'kmaw territory" to "traditional territory" — unceded is load-bearing under the Peace and Friendship Treaties.
- Do not claim Klara serves Mi'kmaw communities. Klara does not claim to advance Mi'kmaw food sovereignty.
- Do not present "routes" and "suggests" as in conflict. They operate at different layers (infrastructure vs decision).
