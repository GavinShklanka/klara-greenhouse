# Business Model

## Routing value, not marketplace volume

The business model is grounded in the value of a good decision routed confidently — and in the partnership channels Klara routes through.

---

<div class="section-divider"><span>What Klara is selling</span></div>

**Decision-routing value** — A household, prosumer, or operator who gets a credible, locally-grounded routed path avoids a costly mistake. Real economic value, even before anything is installed.

**Proposal value** — A structured proposal that reflects real Nova Scotia costs, options, and configurations is worth paying for because it shortens the path to a confident purchase.

**Partnership-channel value** — Routing buyers to appropriate local suppliers and installers, when that data is production-grade, is a routing service with transaction-adjacent value to both sides.

---

<div class="section-divider"><span>Revenue logic (current thinking)</span></div>

| Model | How it works | Status | Tier |
|---|---|---|---|
| Per-proposal fee | User pays for a completed, credible routed proposal | Current prototype structure | <span class="ev-badge ev-B">B</span> |
| Supplier referral | Partner suppliers pay for qualified, high-intent routed referrals | Future — requires supplier data depth | <span class="ev-badge ev-C">C</span> |
| Installer routing | Installers pay for or support access to pre-qualified buyers | Future — requires validated installer network | <span class="ev-badge ev-C">C</span> |
| B2B demand-declaration access | Buyers pay for forward-demand-routing into the operator network | Phase 2 — Function 3 production scale | <span class="ev-badge ev-D">D</span> |

---

<div class="section-divider"><span>What this is NOT</span></div>

- **Not a marketplace** — Klara does not list, compare, or transact products at scale; no transactions clear through the platform
- **Not a subscription tool** — no recurring monitoring or ongoing service in current scope
- **Not a platform play** — no third-party developer ecosystem, no API commerce layer

---

<div class="section-divider"><span>Why the model is narrow right now</span></div>

The model expands as validation proves the routing is credible. A referral to a supplier only has value if the routed recommendation was trusted. That is why validation comes first.

---

> *The business case depends on the routing being right. That's exactly what we're validating.*
