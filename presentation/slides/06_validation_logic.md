# What This Project Needs to Prove

**How we are checking whether Klara is useful, credible, and grounded in Nova Scotia reality.**

This stage is not about whether people think the idea sounds interesting. It is about whether intake, routing, and next-step guidance are clear enough to be trusted.

---

<div class="decision-callout">
  <div class="decision-label">Evidence-tier discipline</div>
  <p>Every claim Klara surfaces carries a tier: <span class="ev-badge ev-A">A</span> directly supported by official data or documented partner confirmation; <span class="ev-badge ev-B">B</span> credible practitioner pattern; <span class="ev-badge ev-C">C</span> reasoned inference; <span class="ev-badge ev-D">D</span> placeholder or speculative. The user sees what is confirmed and what is inference. They decide.</p>
</div>

---

<div class="section-divider"><span>What we are trying to answer</span></div>

1. **Is the problem real in Nova Scotia?** Do households and small operators actually struggle to choose a path, or are we overstating the gap?
2. **Does the intake language make sense?** Can a potential grower move through the questions without confusion or backtracking?
3. **Do the routed paths feel credible?** When someone sees the result, does it feel like a good fit for budget, site, and goals?
4. **What is still missing?** Where does the tool feel too generic, too thin, or unproven?
5. **What should this project pressure-test first?** What needs tightening so the routing becomes more credible and usable?

---

<div class="section-divider"><span>How we are checking it</span></div>

| Channel | What it tests | Tier |
|---|---|---|
| NSCC meetings + follow-up | Scope fit, intake feedback, testing path, service-provider role | <span class="ev-badge ev-A">A</span> |
| Operator interviews | Real decision friction, sizing mistakes, timing/cost issues | <span class="ev-badge ev-B">B</span> |
| Perennia / technical review | Routing credibility, crop-fit logic, terminology | <span class="ev-badge ev-A">A</span> |
| Halifax Seed / sector conversations | Household questions, buyer confusion, seasonal patterns | <span class="ev-badge ev-B">B</span> |
| Intake walkthroughs | Confusing fields, missing questions, completion issues | <span class="ev-badge ev-C">C</span> |

---

> *Known issue, named openly: intake language still needs to align better with how growers actually describe their needs. That is part of the project, not something hidden.*
