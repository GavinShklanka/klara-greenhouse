# Product Today <span class="update-badge">v1.0.4 reframe</span>

## A working routing prototype, with honest scope on what is not yet validated

Klara exists and functions. The routing core handles intake, evidence-tiered options, and proposal output today. Validation deepens what is already built.

---

<div class="section-divider"><span>What the routing core does today</span></div>

- **Structured intake flow** that routes users through site, budget, crop intent, and readiness questions
- **Defined archetypes** that the routing core matches intake responses against
- **Decision-support layer** that surfaces alternatives with explicit tradeoffs (Klara suggests; humans decide — per Landscape v1.0.3 § Decision-support posture)
- **Proposal output** with cost framing readable to a real buyer
- **Payment scaffolding** wired into the proposal flow
- **Nova Scotia–first logic** that routes through local climate, supplier, and channel context — not generic national defaults

---

<div class="section-divider"><span>What is not yet validated</span></div>

These are not failures — they are the reasons this project exists.

| Gap | What it means | Tier |
|---|---|---|
| Vocabulary alignment | Intake language may not match how real growers think and speak | <span class="ev-badge ev-C">C</span> |
| Routing depth | Logic needs pressure-testing against real user scenarios | <span class="ev-badge ev-C">C</span> |
| Stakeholder proof | No formal third-party validation yet — that's what PIVP enables | <span class="ev-badge ev-D">D</span> |
| Local supplier routing data | Supplier and installer data is thin, not yet production-grade | <span class="ev-badge ev-D">D</span> |

---

<div class="section-divider"><span>Why surface the gaps openly</span></div>

The PIVP application is precisely about closing these gaps. Pretending they don't exist undermines the case for structured support. Pretending they're solved already undermines credibility.

---

## Speaker Notes

- Lead with what exists. The working routing prototype is meaningful at this stage; do not undersell it.
- Transition to gaps without apology — "here's what we don't know yet, and here's the plan" is a strong founder frame.
- Hybrid verb live demo: in this slide, "routes" appears in the infrastructure descriptions; "suggests" appears in the decision-support callout. Both are correct because they describe different layers.
- If asked for a demo: have it ready. Do not live-demo unless current stability is high.
- The gap table is also implicitly a statement of what PIVP funding addresses.

<!-- do_not_say -->
## Do Not Say

- Do not say "MVP" unless asked — invites debate about what's minimum.
- Do not over-explain technical architecture; this audience cares about fit and function.
- Do not present gaps as evidence the idea is wrong — they are normal at prototype stage.
- Do not say "recommendation engine" in present tense — Landscape v1.0.3 explicitly disclaims that framing. Use "routing core" or "decision-support layer."
