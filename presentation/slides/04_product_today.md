# Product Today

## A working prototype: not a finished product

Klara exists and functions. What it does today is the honest baseline.

---

### What exists

- **Structured intake flow**, guides users through site, budget, crop intent, and readiness questions
- **Greenhouse archetypes**, defined recommendation categories with local fit logic
- **Decision-support engine**, matches intake responses to viable greenhouse configurations and surfaces alternatives with explicit tradeoffs (Klara suggests; humans decide — per Landscape v1.0.3)
- **Proposal structure**, outputs a readable proposal with cost framing
- **Payment scaffolding**, basic transaction structure exists
- **Nova Scotia-first logic**, routing considers local climate and context, not generic national defaults

---

### What is incomplete or unvalidated

These are not failures, they are the reasons this project exists:

| Gap | What it means |
|-----|---------------|
| Vocabulary alignment | Intake language may not match how real growers think and speak |
| Recommendation depth | Scoring logic needs pressure-testing against real user scenarios |
| Stakeholder proof | No formal third-party validation yet, that's what PIVP enables |
| Local supplier routing | Supplier and installer data is thin, not yet production-grade |

---

### Why present the gaps

Because the PIVP application is precisely about closing them. Pretending they don't exist undermines the case for structured support.

---

> *The prototype proves the approach is buildable. Validation proves it's useful. Reference: Klara Landscape v1.0.3 (2026-04-24) — § Decision-support posture.*

<!-- speaker_notes -->
## Speaker Notes

- Lead with what exists, there's a real working prototype. That's meaningful at this stage.
- Transition to gaps without apology. "Here's what we don't know yet, and here's the plan" is a strong founder frame.
- If they ask for a demo: have it ready, but don't live-demo unless you're confident in current stability.
- The gap table is also an implicit statement of what PIVP funding would address.

<!-- do_not_say -->
## Do Not Say

- Do not say "MVP" unless they ask, the framing invites debate about what's minimum.
- Do not over-explain the technical architecture. This audience cares about fit and function.
- Do not present gaps as problems that indicate the idea is wrong, they're normal at prototype stage.
- Do not say "recommendation engine" in present tense — Landscape v1.0.3 explicitly disclaims that framing. Use "decision-support engine" or "suggests viable paths".
