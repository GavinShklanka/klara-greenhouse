# PIVP Scope

## Layer 1 only: this is a validation and refinement project

What is in scope for this application is deliberately narrow. Narrow is correct.

---

### Layer 1: What PIVP funds (THIS project)

| Focus area | What it means in practice |
|------------|--------------------------|
| **Intake refinement** | Align question language with how real users think and speak |
| **Recommendation refinement** | Stress-test scoring logic against real user scenarios |
| **Proposal clarity** | Make outputs readable and credible to a real buyer |
| **Early user validation** | Structured conversations with Lane 1 and Lane 2 users |
| **Stakeholder proofing** | Gather formal signal from suppliers, NSCC, and sector contacts |
| **NS-specific routing credibility** | Deepen local climate and context logic |

---

### What is NOT in scope for this application

| Out of scope | Why it's out of scope |
|-------------|----------------------|
| Layer 2 (monitoring, crop tracking) | Not validated yet; would dilute Layer 1 focus |
| Layer 3 (deep crop intelligence, follow-up loops) | Future state contingent on Layer 1 success |
| National expansion | Premature, local proof first |
| Marketplace or directory functionality | Different product category; not what this is |

---

### The discipline this requires

Layers 2 and 3 exist in the roadmap. They are not being funded or built here. Any slide, conversation, or document that presents them as current scope is a mistake.

---

> *Scope discipline is credibility. Funders and partners trust founders who know what they are and are not doing.*

## Speaker Notes

- This slide is as much for you as for them. Hold the scope line.
- If they ask about monitoring or crop intelligence: "That's Layer 2. That's not what we're asking for here. We need to prove Layer 1 first."
- If they push on "what's the bigger vision": refer to the roadmap slide, but anchor back to what this application covers.
- The "not in scope" table prevents scope drift in the room.

## Do Not Say

- Do not say "we also plan to add monitoring" in this context, it undercuts the application ask.
- Do not present Layer 2 or 3 to soften the fact that Layer 1 is small. Small and validated beats big and unproven.
- Do not use "phase" language unless you're prepared to explain phase gates.
