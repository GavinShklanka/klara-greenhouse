# Speaker Notes — Master Reference

This file contains cross-slide guidance for running any meeting using this presentation.

---

## General Principles

**Lead with what exists.** The prototype is real. Don't apologize for being early-stage — explain what you've built and why the gaps are what they are.

**Hold the scope line.** Layer 1 is Layer 1. If someone asks about monitoring, crop intelligence, or national expansion — acknowledge it exists in the roadmap, then redirect to what you're actually asking for support on.

**Be honest about what isn't validated.** Funders and institutional partners can tell when a founder is overselling. The credibility of the ask depends on being accurate about what you know and what you don't.

**Don't let the room expand the scope for you.** If someone says "have you thought about also doing X?" — the answer is "yes, that's in the later roadmap. Right now we're focused on proving Layer 1."

## Audience-Specific Notes

### PIVP Application Context

- Lead with the prototype — it distinguishes this application from pure concept-stage submissions
- Validation logic slide is important — PIVP wants to see rigor, not just enthusiasm
- Scope slide matters — they're funding a specific thing, not a vision
- The ask slide should be crisp: this is what we need, this is what it enables

### NSCC Meeting Context

- Open with fit check before pitching — don't assume they know what PIVP or this project is
- Role clarity matters early — NSCC has had uncomfortable partnerships before; pre-empt it
- The user-testing angle is the most natural entry point — it's something they can actually do
- Letter of support is a concrete, low-burden ask — surface it once you've established fit

### Investor / Advisor Context

- Skip the NSCC-specific slides or treat them as context, not pitch
- Lean harder on business model and roadmap
- User lanes framing helps investors understand who the customer actually is
- Be prepared for the "what's the moat?" question — the answer is local data depth and routing credibility, not technology

## Common Questions and How to Answer Them

**"What's the market size?"**
We're not leading with market size at this stage. This is a Nova Scotia-first validation project. The market is the growers in this province who are currently making bad decisions or no decisions at all. We'll have better data on that after validation.

**"Why not just make an app?"**
The intake flow and recommendation engine are the app. The question is whether the logic is right, not whether the interface exists. That's what we're validating.

**"How is this different from asking a greenhouse supplier?"**
Suppliers start from their product catalog. Klara starts from the user's site, budget, and intent. The output is a recommendation that helps the user have a better conversation with a supplier — not a replacement for that conversation.

**"When will you make money?"**
The per-proposal model is already scaffolded. Commercial transactions start when the recommendation is credible enough that users will pay for a proposal. That's what PIVP is funding us to prove.

**"Can this work outside Nova Scotia?"**
The logic is portable. The data isn't. We'll have that conversation after we've proven it works here.
