# NSCC Meeting Brief

**Purpose:** Initial conversation about partnership fit for PIVP Tier 1 application support

**Format:** This brief is for founder preparation — not for distribution to NSCC

---

## What you want to walk away with

1. A clear sense of whether NSCC sees this as a fit for their applied partnership model
2. Understanding of what NSCC engagement would actually look like in practice
3. Clarity on whether a letter of support is achievable and on what timeline
4. Any pre-conditions or milestones NSCC would want to see before formalizing

---

## What NSCC most likely wants to know

- What is the project, in plain language?
- What are you asking from NSCC specifically?
- What's the benefit to NSCC and their programs?
- How much time/resource commitment does this require from their side?
- What's the IP and ownership situation?

---

## Key frames to establish early

**You are the founder.** NSCC is a partner, not a co-founder. The product direction belongs to you.

**This is applied validation, not academic research.** The tool is live and being refined against real users. NSCC's role is to provide access to structured testing environments and institutional signal.

**The ask is bounded.** User-testing support, usability walkthroughs, and potentially a letter of support. Nothing open-ended.

**The relationship can grow.** If this works, there are natural ways to deepen the engagement — but the current ask is specific and limited.

---

## Language to use

- "Applied validation partner" (not "collaborator" or "co-developer")
- "User-testing support" (not "research partnership")
- "Institutional credibility" (not "endorsement" — it implies more than it should at this stage)
- "Structured intake walkthroughs" (specific, not vague)
- "Letter of support for PIVP" (concrete and low-burden)

---

## Red lines

- Do not agree to IP sharing or joint ownership in any form
- Do not agree to academic publication rights without counsel
- Do not let the scope expand into ongoing operational dependency
- Do not promise outcomes you can't control (user numbers, revenue, adoption)

---

## If the meeting goes sideways

**If they say this isn't the right fit:**
"That's helpful to know. What would need to be different about the project for it to fit? Or is there another channel you'd point us toward?"

**If they want a larger role than you're offering:**
"I appreciate the interest — let's close Layer 1 first and then have the broader conversation from a stronger foundation."

**If they ask for things you can't give:**
"That's not something I'm in a position to commit to at this stage. What I can offer is [X]. Is that enough to move forward?"

---

## After the meeting

- Document what was said, not just what was agreed
- Send a follow-up summary within 24 hours
- Be clear about next steps and who owns them
- Do not let "we'll think about it" sit for more than a week without a check-in
